Instagram likes and good mental health two parallel lines?

-By Sonal

Going out for a movie? Or a date? Or a Family trip? The first thing that comes to our mind is that there will be pictures and posts on our social.
In this post, we will see Why the recent announcement from Instagram chief Adam Mosseri to hide Instagram likes is the right choice.
Instagram is highly successful because of the impact it has on people and on young minds especially.
It is worth mentioning that Instagram has connected the globe with just a single click. Undoubtedly, Instagram has a lot to offer from creative insightful motivational quotes, tips, and tricks to outfit hacks. And maybe that is the reason why our teenage generation is loving it.
It’s a tradition for young people nowadays. For an 18-year-old-
• No pictures mean it didn’t happen.
• A post on Instagram validates your fun experience.
• More the likes more popular you are.
This is what most young people believe in. But did you know that for a lot of people the whole concept of Instagram likes and its interconnection with popularity and fame is kind of disturbing? It takes a toll on their mental health.

How the concept of Instagram Likes affects mental state?

A lot of young people have developed a psychology that being liked on social media is important. But the reality is Instagram pictures instills insecurities, promotes dishonesty.
All the pretty pictures and flat lays are faked to capture your attention, to promote a brand they are being paid for.
Pictures representing the desired body figure, skin color, or a better lifestyle can make another feel inferior. It promotes prejudices based upon skin color or a particular body figure. The result? Feeling of not being sufficient and the constant pressure to fit in, to talk cool, and be beautiful.

How Erasing the concept of Instagram likes – The right choice?

Recently Instagram chief announced that Instagram is planning to hide the no of likes one gets on a post. Only the person who posted something will get to know how many likes they got and nobody else. This might be a very small step to revolutionize the way people look up to Instagram.
Likes will not determine your personality.
Hiding the no of likes will definitely-
• Reduce the pressure of being perfect and flawless (It is important to mention that perfection is a myth)
• Teenagers will feel less left out
• No Anxiety and depression associated with likes
• Reduce the pressure of living the best life
• Good mental health and well being

Bottom line

If scrolling through Instagram and looking at other people’s lifestyles makes you think that your life is not happening. Have a second thought about that. Spend less time online. Understand that you are seeing what they want to show you. And it’s not their reality.
Our generation z needs to understand that the mere reality of Instagram. It's high time they wake up and leave the negativity and toxic comparisons behind.
The influence and impact of people would still exist but toxic comparisons will be long gone with the new changes.
